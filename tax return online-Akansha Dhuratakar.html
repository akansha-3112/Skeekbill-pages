What is e-Filing of ITR?


e-Filing of ITR refers to the electronic submission of your Income Tax Return through the official portal of the Income Tax Department.

Whether you choose the online or offline method, you can:
● File your return from anywhere
● Track status updates
● Get faster refunds
● Avoid penalties and late fees

How to File Tax Return Online?


You can file your tax return online in two ways:

● Online Mode (Direct Entry on Portal)

  1. Visit: https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal
  2. Login using your PAN as user ID and password.
  3. Go to: e-File > Income Tax Returns > File Income Tax Return
  4. Select:
    • Assessment Year
    • Mode of Filing: Online
    • ITR Form based on your income type
    • Type of Assessee: Individual / HUF / Others
  5. Click on Start Filing
  6. Fill all mandatory fields
  7. Preview return > Validate return
  8. Proceed to verification (explained below)

Tip: You can save a draft and resume later in case of interruption.

● Offline Mode (JSON Upload via Utility Tool)

  1. Download the appropriate ITR utility (Excel or Java) from the portal.
  2. Extract and run the utility.
  3. Fill in your data manually or import pre-filled JSON.
  4. Validate, then generate the JSON file.
  5. Login to the e-Filing Portal and upload the JSON file.
  6. Complete the verification process.

Verification Methods After e-Filing

Your ITR filing is incomplete until it's verified.
Choose from the following options:

  • E-Verify Now:
    ● OTP on Aadhaar-linked mobile
    ● Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

  • EVC via:
    ● Net Banking
    ● Bank Account
    ● Demat Account

  • E-Verify Later:
    ● Log in > e-file > e-Verify Return
    ● Or use the home page e-verify return feature

  • ITR-V (Physical Verification):
    ● Send the signed ITR-V to Centralized Processing Center, Income Tax Department, Bengaluru – 560500 via speed post

Which ITR Form Should I Choose?

ITR Form Applicable To
ITR-1 Salaried individuals with income less than ₹50 lakh
ITR-2 Individuals with capital gains or foreign income
ITR-3 Professionals and business owners
ITR-4 Presumptive income (freelancers/traders)
ITR-5 to ITR-7 LLPs, firms, companies, charitable institutions

Note: Selecting the wrong form can lead to rejection of your return.

Who Must File Income Tax Return Online?

E-filing is mandatory if:


  • Your income exceeds the basic exemption limit


  • Your business turnover exceeds ₹60 lakh


  • You’ve spent ₹2 lakh or more on foreign travel


  • Your electricity bill exceeds ₹1 lakh


  • You hold foreign assets or signing authority abroad
